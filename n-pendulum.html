What's actually being solved

Each bob's motion depends on the position and velocity of every other bob in the chain — a moving tip yanks on the whole rope. The equations below come straight out of the Lagrangian for n point masses on massless rigid links, one per bob, solved simultaneously at every frame.

Mk is the combined mass of bob k and everything hanging past it. There's no small-angle approximation anywhere — this is the full nonlinear system, integrated with 4th-order Runge–Kutta.

Built for exploration, not prediction — past roughly 15 links, the same sensitivity that makes this interesting also means floating-point rounding is enough to send the simulation and "reality" down different paths within seconds. That's not a bug in the code; it's the content of the theorem. 40 links is the hard ceiling, mostly out of mercy for your CPU.
